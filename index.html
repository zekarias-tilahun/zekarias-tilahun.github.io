About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in Machine Learning at KTH Royal Institute of Technology. I completed my PhD in Computer Science with a specialization in Machine Learning under the supervision of Prof. Montresor.

My research broadly spans Machine Learning, Graph Analytics, and Natural Language Processing, with particular emphasis on Graph-based Machine Learning methods.

Currently, my work focuses on Graph Embedding (also known as Graph Representation Learning), building upon my doctoral research. I explore cutting-edge topics including Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), Contextualized Graph Representation Learning, and Self-supervised GNNs.

Research Interests

Graph Neural Networks

Deep learning architectures for graph-structured data, including message passing networks and spectral methods.

Graph Embedding

Learning low-dimensional representations of graphs preserving structural and semantic properties.

Self-Supervised Learning

Contextualized and self-supervised approaches for graph representation learning without labeled data.
